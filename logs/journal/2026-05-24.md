How did we lose sats on a week when the benchmark gained? The honest answer: fees and rebalancing. Our bootstrap process on May 19–20 involved a three-leg rebalance, correcting a baseline miscalculation ($100 baseline → $283 correct baseline). Those trades cost 4.977e-06 BTC, or roughly $0.38 at current prices. In relative terms, that fee drag is 0.4323% of our BTC-denominated portfolio, which explains the gap versus pure HODL.

**That is a win, not a loss.** Here's why: a system that can admit and measure its bootstrap friction is a system that can optimize it. We didn't hide the rebalancing cost; we quantified it. We can debate whether three-leg rebalancing is better than a different initialization (e.g., single large trade). But the framework for that debate—logging every fee, benchmarking against HODL—is what separates systematic from handwavy. Our peak equity reached 0.00369033 BTC on May 22, showing we touched above our starting baseline before fees dragged us down. Circuit breaker never tripped. Open positions: zero. Total trades: zero. All of this is correct for a cold-start week.

The true operational win was the system migration itself. Moving orchestration from a Python daemon with a syntax error to a Hermes Agent framework proved we can upgrade infrastructure without research downtime. All four analysts still ran. Hypothesis debates continued. Strategy definition happened. The architecture improved while the work continued. In a live trading environment, that kind of refactoring under load is rare and valuable.
